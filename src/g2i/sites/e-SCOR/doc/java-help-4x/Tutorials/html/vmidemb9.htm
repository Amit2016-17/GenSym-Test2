Summary: Optimizing the Central Distributor Model

To optimize the central distributor model, you:

Determine optimal replenishment levels for each distribution site by:
- Optimizing replenishment levels for the worst case scenario, which involves setting order size and delivery times to their maximum values in all cases, then increasing replenishment levels to obtain at least a 90% on-time performance.
- Minimizing inventory days of supply, which involves setting order size and delivery times back to their original levels, then reducing replenishment levels to minimize inventory days of supply while maintaining 100% on-time performance.
Implementing vendor-managed inventory control by reducing delivery times and corresponding replenishment levels, then comparing the total inventory days of supply for the central distributor and the three distribution sites with that of the optimized simulation where each site owns its own inventory.
Analyzing opportunity cost of providing different service levels of on-time performance to help make key business decisions around contract terms and pricing for different customers.
Use the Scenario Manager to run multiple simulations.
