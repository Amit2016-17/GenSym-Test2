Assigning Products to Roles: Simplest Model

Next, you specify which roles process which products by assigning each product specification in the product hierarchy to one or more roles in the supply chain. In the simplest model, both the Base Manufacturer and Consumer roles process the product family.

For Base Manufacturer, Distributor, and Manufacturer roles, you assign the product specification that the role delivers. For a Consumer role, you assign the product specification that the role sources.

When you assign a product specification to a role, e-SCOR creates the following objects, as appropriate for the role:

Source product - The product that a buyer role sources.
Delivery product - The product that a supplier role delivers.

For example, when you assign a product specification to a Base Manufacturer role, e-SCOR creates a delivery product, and when you assign a product specification to a Consumer role, e-SCOR creates a source product.

Distributor and Manufacturer roles define both source and delivery products, as you will learn later in this tutorial.

e-SCOR places source and delivery products in the Products pool on the role detail. A pool is another type of container in which e-SCOR places objects that it creates, for example, transient objects such as orders that it creates and deletes when the simulation runs.

Together, source and delivery products are called product composites.

To assign products to roles for the simplest model:

1 Display the properties dialog for the Base Manufacturer role.
2 Click the Products tab.
e-SCOR displays a tree representation of the product hierarchy associated with the model. In this case, the product hierarchy consists of a single product specification. In a real model, the tree representation would consists of a finished product and its components.
To assign a product to a role, select the product specification in the tree.
3 Click Product Family and apply.
The Product Family is now a delivery product of the Base Manufacturer role.
4 Display the properties dialog for the Consumer role, click the Products tab, and click Product Family.

The Product Family is now a source product of the Consumer role.

To show which products are assigned to which roles:

Choose Show Products on a role or Show Roles on a product specification.

You should see indicator arrows pointing to the assigned roles or products, as appropriate.

Tip: To remove individual indicator arrows, choose Hide Indicator on the arrow. Alternatively, choose Clear Indicators from the Simulation menu or click the equivalent toolbar button: The Scenario must be active before this menu choice is available. To activate the Scenario, choose Activate from the Simulation menu or click the equivalent toolbar button:

You will view and configure the product composites that e-SCOR creates later in this tutorial.
