Viewing Base Manufacturer Role Details

The Base Manufacturer role detail includes these process categories, by default:

Mb: Make Base Manufacturer category, which defines the cost of raw materials in the supply chain and the manufacturing time for making batches.
P3: Plan Make category, which defines the frequency with which the role creates build orders, which are orders for delivery products that the role sends to manufacturing, and implicitly determines when the role delivers those products.
D1: Deliver Stocked Product category, which defines the amount of time it takes to enter an order for stock products, the criteria the role uses to determine which orders to deliver first, and various timing parameters related to delivering products.
ED: Enable Delivery category, which is responsible for receiving orders from and establishing contracts with downstream buyers for delivery products. The ED category has no configurable parameters.

A Base Manufacturer role requires the Mb category as its Make category. Manufacturer roles include the M1: Make-to-Stock category as the default Make category. You can also include the M2: Make-to-Order and/or the M3: Engineer-to-Order categories on the role detail to use alternative planning strategies.

Base Manufacturer and Manufacturer roles requires the P3: Plan Make category on the role detail for make planning. Make planning determines the number of finished products a manufacturer role makes and the frequency with which it sends build orders to manufacturing.

Base Manufacturer, Distributor, and Manufacturer roles include the D1: Deliver Stocked Product category as the default Deliver category. You can also include the D2: Deliver Make-to-Order Product and/or the D3: Deliver Engineer-to-Order Product categories on the role detail to use alternative planning strategies.

Base Manufacturer, Distributor, and Manufacturer roles all require the ED: Enable Delivery categories on the role detail.

To view role details:

Choose Show Detail on the Base Manufacturer role.
