Viewing Source Product Metrics: Consumer Role

The source product of a buyer role computes:

The average purchase cost for the source product, based on the Net Selling Price of each upstream supplier's delivery product.
Total number of source products ordered and received since the start of the simulation, which should be the same as the Quantity to Deliver and Quantity Shipped for the delivery product.
The average fulfillment time, in days, from when a buyer orders the source product to when the buyer receives product shipments, which should be the same as the Order Fulfillment Lead Time (Days) for the delivery product (Received Average Fulfillment Time).
The number of source products the buyer has received but not yet placed in inventory, and is, thus, incoming (Incoming Amount).
The number of source products a buyer has ordered but suppliers have not yet delivered (On Order).

Inventory Level, Potential Inventory Level, Inventory Status, and Awaiting Order Total are not relevant for source products of a Consumer role.

Normally, the source product of a buyer role also computes the Incoming Amount, which is the total number of source products a buyer has received but has not yet put into inventory, and the Work in Progress, which is the total number of source products a buyer has taken from inventory and is waiting to use to manufacture finished products and/or deliver product shipments. However, because a Consumer role does not keep an inventory, it does not compute these metrics.

To view source product metrics for the Consumer role:

While the simulation is paused, display the properties dialog for the source product in the Products pool on the Consumer role detail and view these metrics:
