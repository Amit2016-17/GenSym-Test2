Financial Process for Immediate Payments

Many businesses operate using a financial model that requires immediate payments. For example, many e-commerce and mail order businesses use this model, where the supplier requires that the buyer make a payment at the same time that it places an order. In this model, suppliers do not send invoices and do not have financial bookings or outstanding payments, and buyers do not incur financial obligations. Instead, the buyer places an order and sends a payment simultaneously.

1 Generate order/Send payment.
The Source category of the buyer generates an order. For orders that originate in a Consumer role, the Sc: Source Consumer category computes the order cost by multiplying its Order Size parameter by the Purchase Cost metric of the role's source product.
For orders that originate in a Distributor or Manufacturer role, the Source category computes the order cost by multiplying the size of the replenishment order, which it receives from the Source Planning process, by the Purchase Cost metric.
For information on source planning, see Source Planning Process.
Simultaneous with generating the order, the Source category of the buyer sends the payment, which increments the Financial Payments Total metric for the Source category and the buyer role.
The buyer does not compute the Financial Obligations metric, and the supplier does not compute the Financial Bookings metric.
The Source category applies the Supply Chain Finance cost parameter to its Supply Chain Finance costs incurred metric and to the aggregate cost metrics associated with the buyer role.
2 Enter order.
The Deliver category of the supplier enters the order, which is part of the order-processing and fulfillment process, described in Order/Product Process.
3 Send shipment.
The Deliver category of the supplier sends the shipment, which is part of the order-processing and fulfillment process.
The buyer does not compute the Financial Obligations metric, and the supplier does not compute the Financial Bookings metric.
The supplier does not generate an invoice, and the buyer role does not receive an invoice.
4 Receive shipment.
The Source category of the buyer receives the shipment as part of the order-processing and fulfillment process.
5 Receive payment.
When the supplier receives the payment, the Deliver category increments the Financial Collections Total metric for the Deliver category and for the supplier role.
The Deliver category does not compute the Financial Outstanding metric.
The Deliver category applies the Customer Collections cost parameter to its Customer Accounting costs incurred metric and to the aggregate costs for the role.
