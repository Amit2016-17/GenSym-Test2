e-SCOR User's Guide
Topic - What Happens When the Simulation Runs

Parameters and Metrics by Function

SCOR categorizes parameters and metrics into several functional groups, which help you to understand their purpose and meaning:

Flexibility and responsiveness (Flex. & respons.) - Parameters and metrics related to the performance of the model with respect to processes and transactions.
Reliability - Parameters and metrics related to the effectiveness of the processes. Because e-SCOR assumes perfect transactions, it does not compute many reliability metrics.
Assets and financials- Parameters and metrics that relate to the cost and sale of inventory, such as the inventory level for individual products and the days of supply for each role; financial payments by buyers and financial collections by suppliers; and asset measurements of inventory and other related role summarizations, such as cash-to-cash cycle time, asset turns, and cash flow.
Costs - Parameters and metrics that relate to the cost of doing business that result from certain transactions, such as the cost of entering an order and of shipping products.

e-SCOR defines two additional categories of parameters and metrics:

Simulation execution - Parameters and metrics that relate to the status of the currently running simulation.
Model configuration - Parameters and metrics that relate to how the model behaves.

When you run the simulation, each process uses parameters and computes metrics that relate to one or more of these functional groups. For example, certain steps perform modeling tasks and compute performance metrics; other steps compute financial metrics; other steps contribute to or compute supply-chain costs; and other steps cause the simulation clock to advance.
