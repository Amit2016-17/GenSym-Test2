Role Verification Process

When a simulation starts, e-SCOR runs the Role Verification process, which takes a total of two seconds of simulation time, as this diagram illustrates:

1 Send purchase request.
The ES: Enable Source category of the buyer sends a purchase request to each upstream supplier to which it is connected to determine which supplier(s) can deliver its source product. The buyer performs this step for each of its source products.
2 Send purchase response.
If a supplier can deliver the requested product, the ED: Enable Delivery category of the supplier responds by sending a purchase response back to the buyer. The buyer stores the purchase response in its Responses pool on the role detail. If a supplier role cannot deliver the requested product, it does not respond to the purchase request. This step occurs when the simulation clock is at zero.
3 Receive and verify responses.
3.1 The ES category ensures that it has purchase responses for all purchase requests. It also verifies and determines a valid pull/push configuration between roles. This step occurs when the simulation clock is 1 second.
3.2 The ES category increments its Contracts Established metric by one.
4 Send purchase award.
The buyer issues a purchase award to each supplier that sends a purchase response. The ES category of the buyer places the purchase award in its Buyer Awards pool on the role detail. It then creates a copy of the purchase award and sends it to each supplier that sent a purchase response.
5 Save purchase award.
The Deliver category increments it Contracts Established metric by one.
The Deliver category of the supplier stores the purchase awards in the Supplier Awards pool on its detail. This step is complete when the simulation clock is 2 seconds.
Note: Because the Role Verification process takes 2 seconds of simulation time to complete, you should configure all timing initialization parameters to begin two or more seconds after the start time of the simulation. These parameters include Initial Plan Delay and Initial Contract Delay; neither of these parameters should have a value of less than 2 seconds.
