Example: Amount to Order is the Amount Needed to Maintain Current Inventory

Here is an example where the Amount to Order is the amount that is needed to maintain desired inventory levels, given current orders, because this amount is greater than the Minimum Reorder Quantity:

Inventory Level 120
Replenishment Threshold 100
On Order 30
Awaiting Order Total 90
Minimum Reorder Quantity 30

120 - 100 + 30 = 50 (Inventory Level - Replenishment Threshold +
                     On Order)

50 < 90 (50 < Awaiting Order Total)

100 - 120 - 30 + 90 = 40 (Replenishment Threshold - Inventory Level -
                          On Order + Awaiting Order Total)

max [30, 40] (max [Minimum Reorder Quantity, 40] therefore:

Amount to Order = 40
