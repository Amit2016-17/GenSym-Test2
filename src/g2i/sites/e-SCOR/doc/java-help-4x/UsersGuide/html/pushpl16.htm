Topic - Using Pull and Push Planning Modes

Configuring the Amount to Push When Components are Shared

Suppose a buyer is delivering multiple products that share one or more components and upstream suppliers are pushing these products downstream onto the buyer. By default, the buyer uses these components to deliver finished products in a random order. You can configure the Product Preference parameter of the buyer's delivery product to determine which components the buyer uses first.

The Product Preference is a number that represents the priority of the supplier's delivery product, where the lower the number the higher the priority

If the number of products that the upstream supplier pushes exceeds the requirements of the downstream buyer, the buyer places the excess in inventory, which the buyer might or might not use. If the upstream supplier does not push enough products onto the buyer, the buyer cannot deliver the desired number of finished products.

To illustrate, suppose your model has a Distributor role that delivers two finished products, P1 and P2, and sources three components, A, B, and C. The product specification for P1 requires components A and C, and the product specification for P2 requires components B and C; therefore, component C is shared.

Assume you configure the Product Preference for P1 to be 1 and the Product Preference for P2 to be 2. This means the component requirements for P1 determine the number of C components the supplier pushes downstream.

Suppose the suppliers for products A, B, and C attempt to push their products downstream, given a Push Amount of 50, 50, and 75, respectively.

Given this configuration, the questions are:

How many units of component C does product P1 use compared with product P2?
How many units of product P1 and P2 does the Distributor role deliver?
How many units of component B actually does the Distributor role actually use?

The results are as follows:

The Distributor role uses 50 units of component C to assemble product P1 and only 25 units of component C to assemble product P2, based on Product Preference.
The Distributor role delivers 50 units of product P1, but it delivers only 25 units of product P2, because that is all it can assemble from the remaining units of component C.
Base Manufacturer B pushes 50 units of component B downstream; however, the Distributor role only uses 25 units to assemble product P2. The rest go into inventory.

To configure the amount to push given shared components:

1 Show the Products pool detail of the buyer role that is downstream of the supplier roles whose Push Amount you configured.
2 Display the properties dialog, click the Product tab, and configure the Product Preference parameter of each delivery product to determine the relative priority of each product, which determines the amount of each source product the buyer actually uses, given the pushed amount.
