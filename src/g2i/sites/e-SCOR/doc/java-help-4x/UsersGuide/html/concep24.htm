Product Hierarchy

A product hierarchy consists of:

A high-level product specification, called a finished product.
One or more lower-level product specifications, called components.

A product specification that contains no other product specifications is called a base component. A product specification that is the end product of the supply chain is called a consumer product.

The following figure shows two different product hierarchies. On the right is the product hierarchy for a laptop finished product, which consists of an electronics component and a case component. On the left is the product hierarchy for a computer, which consist of a monitor, a keyboard, and a CPU component, where the CPU consists of an electronics and a case component. The product hierarchy can describe as many levels of detail as needed.

You can create one or more product hierarchies, as needed, to describe the structure of each consumer product that your supply chain sources and delivers.

Each role sources, makes, and/or delivers one or more products in the product hierarchy, as follows:

A Base Manufacturer role makes and delivers components or finished products, which are raw materials for the overall supply chain.
A Distributor role sources components and delivers finished products as kits, sources and delivers components, and/or sources and delivers finished products.
A Manufacturer role sources components, and makes and delivers finished products.
A Consumer role sources components or finished products from the supplier directly upstream.
