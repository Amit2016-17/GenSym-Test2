Disconnecting the Path Between Roles

When you disconnect two roles, e-SCOR deletes the connection, leaving stubs with junctions on each role. Stubs with junctions behave somewhat differently than stubs without junctions. To use the default behavior when connecting stubs, remove the junction from the end of a stub.

To disconnect two roles:

Choose Delete on the path between two roles.

The stubs with junctions look like this:

When you move a role whose stubs have junctions, the stubs do not move with the role like they do when the stubs have no junctions. Instead, the stubs reconfigure themselves on the role.

To work with stubs that have junctions:

Drag the junction on the stub while holding down the mouse button, then release the mouse button.

For example, to move the stub on the role, drag the junction on the stub to a new location on the role. To delete the stub with a junction, drag it into the role. To connect two roles, drag the stub with a junction into another role.

Note: We do not recommend connecting stubs with junctions to other stubs.

Often, it is easier to work with stubs without the junction on the end.

To remove junctions on the end of stubs:

Choose Delete on an individual stub or select a region that includes multiple junctions and choose Delete.
