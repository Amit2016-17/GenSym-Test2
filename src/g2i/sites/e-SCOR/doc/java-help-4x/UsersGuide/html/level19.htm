Creating a Product Hierarchy with a Single Product Specification

The product hierarchy for the simplest model consists of a single product specification, which represents two products. To model this, you create a single product specification and configure its Product Quantity parameter to be 2. You use this technique when each product in a product family behaves similarly in terms of its Level 2 SCOR parameters and when you do not need to track metrics for each product separately.

This topic describes how to create a product hierarchy that consists of a single product specification. The next topic shows how to create a product hierarchy with components.

Caution: We recommend that you configure the product hierarchy before you create your supply chain and assign products to roles.

To create a product hierarchy with a single product specification:

1 Display the e-SCOR toolbar and click the Products tab.
The Products tab contains a Product Specification and a Product Structure, as follows:

You typically place a product specification on the detail of a product structure, however, that is not a requirement.
2 Place a Product Structure on your model detail, display its properties dialog, then edit its Label parameter.
For shortcuts on displaying the properties dialog for items on a workspace, see Configuring the Model Environment.
3 Show the detail of the product structure.
You create the product hierarchy on this detail.
4 Place a Product Specification on the product structure detail.
5 Configure the Product Name/SKU parameter for the product specification.
You can think of the label as the SKU number for the product, which should be unique. For example, the product name for the simplest model might be Product Family.
6 Configure the Product Quantity parameter of the product specification to be the number of physical products that this product specification represents.

For example, the Product Family might represent 2 physical products.
