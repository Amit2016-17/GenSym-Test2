Overview

Introduction

SymCure is a tool for fault management that performs the following functions:

  • .Address the full life cycle for fault management, including fault isolation, impact analysis, diagnostic testing, and recovery from failure.  

  • .Provide a powerful and elegant model-based framework to specify, organize and store the diagnosis knowledge in the form of a persistent, generic, class-level, graphical, fault propagation model library.  

  • .Perform root cause diagnosis and impact analysis by combining the generic, class-level, graphical fault propagation models with specific domain information and asynchronous events at run time. 

  • .Initiate the execution of diagnostic tests for isolating faults and corrective actions to recover from system failures. 

  • .Notify results of its diagnostic reasoning to external systems using messages and other suitable means. 

  • .Perform impact analysis to predict the effects of problems. 

  • .SymCure’s modeling language must be extensible to facilitate building correct generic fault models in any and every possible situation. 

 

SymCure is aimed at addressing the following problems:

  • .Improve application, equipment, and service availability by  

  • .providing faster identification of root causes of problems,  

  • .helping operators focus on root causes faster, 

  • .predicting impacts of underlying failures before they are manifested, and 

  • .enabling rapid response to detect, test, diagnose, fix, and restore normal operation. 

  • .Automate operator actions such as tests and recovery procedures, allowing the business to grow its equipment, processes, and services, without increasing the number of supervisory operators. 

  • .Reduce alarm flooding by providing concise diagnostic summaries of problems to replace numerous raw alarms and messages which overload an operator with redundant information. 

 

In addition to addressing the critical needs for diagnosis management, the use of SymCure will provide the following benefits.

  • .A standardized methodology and approach for solving these problems in a ‘generic’ way suitable for construction of libraries which can be applied with minimal custom effort at every site. 

  • .The definition of generic, class level fault propagation models in an object-oriented fashion. SymCure will automatically recognize and adapt to topological and status changes in the domain networks.  Hence, there is no need to change or recompile the models for changes in system configurations. 

 

The product is believed to have broad applicability across many industries. The domain modeling technique is essentially a generic (class-level) version of Failure Modes and Effects Analysis (FMEA) and testability analysis, applied for use on-line.  This approach is widely used in the electronics and aerospace industries already, and is now being applied to network design management as well.  Causal directed graph and model-based approaches that represent causal relationships between faults and their effects have received wide attention for problems in manufacturing, refining, and nuclear power industries.

Purpose

The purpose of this document is to list the requirements for the plug-in module including a phased in delivery approach and describe key design issues and implementation issues.  The intended audience for this document includes the following:

  • .potential users of the product, such as consultants, solution engineers, and end users; 

  • .managers involved in the marketing or management of the product;  

  • .people with experience in the target markets, such as strategic sales;  

  • .technical writers responsible for documentation; 

  • .trainers responsible for developing courses for the product; and  

  • .other product developers in need to understand the product evolution and key design issues
